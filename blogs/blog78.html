Do’s and Don’ts – An ultimate guide to the Liposuction recovery procedure

Overview

Are you planning for Liposuction surgery? So many questions would have popped into your mind: Will the surgery be successful? How will your skin be post-surgery? How much time would it take to recover? All your concerns will be addressed right away in this article.

It is no surprise that Liposuction helps reduce or remove stubborn fat cells from the body. If you’re aiming for an easy body contouring and reshaping method, Liposuction surgery can be the right approach. Liposuction renders many advantages over other surgeries while being a relatively simple process to undergo and recover. It is a surgical procedure that removes excess fat via a thin tube called a cannula attached to a vacuum.

Apparently, Liposuction is a safe and popular cosmetic surgery procedure, but it does need some special attention post-surgery. The recovery process can become easy when one knows what to expect and how to get back. Now, let’s get started to uncover the recovery process and make it simpler for aspiring patients like you.

3 things you must consider

  • Liposuction is not a magic wand

You must realize that Liposuction is not ideal for weight loss, while it might help you get rid of the fat pockets forming in your body. It removes the external fat completely but can’t do anything to the internal fat which still needs you to diet and exercise post the procedure. Guard your expectations and be realistic. Everything takes time, especially the recovery process associated with surgeries. So, what you can do is consult a Board-Certified Plastic Surgeon that guides you throughout your surgical procedure. 

  • Consult an experienced, Board-Certified Plastic Surgeon

One is greatly advised to consult a board-certified surgeon before and after surgery. As it is cosmetic surgery, it is liable to have some risks. You must be unclouded about every single query you have. That’s the only way to a hassle-free recovery. You cannot recover soon unless you are clear about the process.

  • The recovery process depends on certain factors.

It is vital to understand that the recovery process varies from person to person. Not everyone gets the same desired results. Liposuction depends on an individual’s overall health, medical history, and of course age! One needs to do complete research beforehand. Well, to know if you’re an ideal candidate for Liposuction, you must keep these things in your mind.

  • Liposuction is for a patient that’s nearly within 30% of their ideal body weight,
  • Someone possessing firm, good elastic skin,
  • A patient with no smoking addictions

So, before planning for the surgery, make sure you are the ideal candidate. And, nobody can guide you better than a Board-Certified Plastic Surgeon.

We’re unlocking the recovery procedure!

You might be wondering when to go home after Liposuction. Liposuction is typically an outpatient procedure. Most often, patients are allowed to go back home on the same day. Once the effect of anesthesia wears off, the patient is told to rest and can go home within an hour. And, for the patients who went through a significant amount of fat cell removal, are instructed to stay overnight at the hospital. When it comes to going home while you are the only driver, it is well-advised to let another person drive. You are likely to feel the superfluous effects of anesthesia or treatments.

The first three days post-surgery.

The patient is instructed to wear compression garments or elastic bandages, which one needs to follow arduously. These garments aid in contouring your body and reduce the amount of swelling while giving a smoother result. If you have some drains around the surgical sites, your surgeon will tell you how the drain will vanish and what care you should take. You are likely to feel some discomfort or soreness around your treatment areas. But don’t worry! Your surgeon will prescribe you medicines to relieve pain and help you recover soon. It is highly recommended to follow your schedule strictly. You should limit your activities to get more rest. The first week after Liposuction is a great time to loosen up and binge-watch your favorite shows.

Weeks 1-2 after Liposuction

After the initial days of surgery, your pain and discomfort will decrease considerably. Although, the general advice for everyone is to take a week off, then gradually get back to the daily schedules. The truth is the recovery process works differently for all.

Weeks 3-5 post-surgery

When you’re about a month, you will feel almost nil pain. Understand that swelling or bruising may take some more time to heal. You may start seeing a difference in the size of the treated area; it will take a few months to get your desired results. Nearly after 4 weeks, you can recommence beginner exercises while avoiding heavy activities.

Week 6 and beyond

Now is the time when a majority of your swelling might have subsided. Persistent swelling may last a bit longer. Wearing compression garments is not required at this point. Additionally, you can full-fledged resume your daily chores unless advised by your surgeon. In the meanwhile, you will start noticing a visible difference in treated areas.

Now that you know what things you must consider pre-surgery, we shall move ahead to uncover the Do’s and Don’t after the surgical process.

Do’s after surgery

Here are a few things you must consider to strengthen your natural healing process.

  • Maintain a healthy lifestyle: Good nutrition is vital for any surgical process. In order to reduce inflammation, you must include protein-rich, Vitamin-C enriched foods in your diet plan. Also, include fiber-containing and iron-rich foods. The healthier you become, the faster you can heal. So, plan your meals accordingly, to get one step close to recovery. Make sure you drink plenty of water and stay hydrated.
  • Be gentle on your activities, take short walks.

During recovery, it is not advisable to do heavy workouts. Instead, you can take short walks without straining your body.

  • Take it easy

You can wear comfortable and loose clothes to avoid skin irritation. Catch up on your favorite book and rest for the initial days after your surgery.

  • Take your medicines on time

It is essential to take medications properly, otherwise, it will slow your recovery process.

  • Visit surgeon for daily checkups

Even if you feel the slightest of change, do visit your surgeon. Also, regularly visit your doctor to ensure the proper recovery procedure.

Don’ts after surgery

  • Avoid certain foods

Avoid foods that contain more sugar, oil, and salt. Make sure you don’t consume any kind of junk food.

  • Avoid heavy-workouts

You can surely take short walks, but avoid heavy exercising to recover fast.

  • Avoid smoking or drinking

As mentioned earlier, Liposuction does not perform well on smokers, so it is likely advised to quit smoking and drinking for better healing.

Final thoughts

Liposuction can be a life-changer for many people. It can help you feel more confident about your appearance. Once you start understanding the recovery process, and how it is meant to be, you can get a clearer picture of whether or not to undergo a Liposuction surgery. Make sure you do your research and consult a surgeon beforehand.
